A Tribute to Norm Macdonald

Some Facts about Norm

  • Norm Macdonald made his first stand-up debut on David Letterman's show in 1990, and was also the last stand-up act seen on Letterman's The Late Show.
  • Norm Macdonald grew up in rural Canada before persuing his career as a stand-up comedian.
  • Norm is often regarded to as a comedian's comedian.
  • Norm is dubbed by Conan O' Brien as one of the best guests he has ever had.
  • When Norm got cancer, he kept his condition private, only letting a very small circle of people in his life know, ensuring that the public looks at him not with pity. He did so for 9 years.
  • Norm, in his life, struggled with gambling addiction.
  • In a celebrity roast, when Norm was tasked to roast his friend, Bob Saget, he purposefully chose to use jokes from an old joke book given to him by his father, so that he did not have to throw harsh insults towards his friend.
  • Comedian, Chevy Chase regards Norm as the only other anchorman to do right by Weekend Update on Saturday Night Live.
